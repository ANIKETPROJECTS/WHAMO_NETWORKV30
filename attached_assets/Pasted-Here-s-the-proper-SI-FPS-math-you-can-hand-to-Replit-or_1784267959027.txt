**1 ft = 0.3048 m** (exact, by international definition)
**1 m = 3.28084 ft**

## Derived conversions needed for this panel

| Quantity | Dimension | SI → FPS | FPS → SI |
|---|---|---|---|
| Length (E, D, Top/Bottom Elevation, Diameter) | L | value_ft = value_m × 3.28084 | value_m = value_ft × 0.3048 |
| Area (A) | L² | value_ft² = value_m² × (3.28084)² = value_m² × 10.7639 | value_m² = value_ft² × (0.3048)² = value_ft² × 0.092903 |
| Velocity (Celerity) | L/T | value_ft/s = value_m/s × 3.28084 | value_m/s = value_ft/s × 0.3048 |

That's actually the safer implementation than converting A directly, because it avoids compounding rounding errors.
